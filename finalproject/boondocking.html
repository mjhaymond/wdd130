Boondock Camping For Beginners

The definition of boondock camping, is camping off-the-grid with an RV, but we like to include tent camping this definition as well. It is also sometimes referred to as “dry camping”. This means any time you go camping without water, sewer, or electrical connections. You are camping in unestablished areas and it is important to come prepared.

This website will get you started with what you need to know to in order to go boondock camping. With a little preperation and planning ahead, boondock camping can be a fun and relaxing escape from the business of life, as well as budget friendly to your bank account!

Tent camping vs RV/Travel trailer camping

RV and Trailer-Camping

RV and Trailer camping are the easiest forms of Boondock camping because essentially the RV is a house on wheels and it holds everything you need, from the kitchen sink and stove to the toilet and shower. This is the most preferred form of Boondock camping because it makes everything very convenient and comfortable. Even though the RV holds all the needed conveniences you might need, you still must be prepared because there are no electrical, water, or sewer hookups. You are camping off the grid and you’ll need to think about how an RV or Camper Trailer will function differently without these conveniences.

Tent-Camping

Tent-camping off the grid can be just as enjoyable as RV camping, but it does require more planning and gear to create the kind of conveniences and comforts that the RV holds. Depending on how much work you want to put into it, you can make your camp very simple and do without or you can bring all the conveniences with you. Things such as an air mattress can make sleeping in a tent very comfortable, or you can use a simple ground pad. A small shower tent can be turned into a bathroom with a bucket, toilet seat, and biodegradable toilet bags, or you can go without and get comfortable with nature! Your level of preparation is up to you.
